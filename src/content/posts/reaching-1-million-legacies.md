When we launched LastRite eighteen months ago, our founding team had a single goal: to build something worthy of the stories people trusted us with. We did not have a launch metric in mind. We did not put "1 million users" on a whiteboard and treat it as a north star.

But this week, the one millionth legacy was created on our platform. And we wanted to take a moment to say what that number means — and, more importantly, what it doesn't mean.

## What the Number Represents

One million legacies means one million people, somewhere in the world, decided that a life mattered enough to document carefully. It means families in Lagos and Los Angeles and Lahore found a way to preserve something that might otherwise have been lost. It means obituaries that will still be readable in fifty years, voice recordings that grandchildren not yet born will one day hear, photographs that will outlast every device they were originally stored on.

It means a lot of grief, honestly. You cannot reach a million memorials without understanding that each one represents a specific absence — a chair that is no longer filled, a phone that no longer rings, a name that has moved from present tense to past.

We hold that weight seriously.

## What the Number Doesn't Represent

It does not represent the end of a journey, or a signal to pivot toward growth at any cost. The companies that chase metrics above all else tend to make decisions that serve the metric rather than the people behind it. We have seen that pattern play out across the industry, and we are committed to not repeating it.

Our next million legacies will be built on the same principles as our first: privacy by design, no advertising, sustainable pricing, and a genuine commitment to the communities that use our platform.

## A Note of Thanks

To everyone who has shared feedback, reported bugs, told a friend, or simply trusted us with something irreplaceable: thank you. You are the reason the product has gotten better every month. You are the reason we show up.

The work of building a platform that is worthy of human memory is never finished. But milestones like this remind us why it matters.

Here's to the next million.
