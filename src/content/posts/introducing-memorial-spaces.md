Grief rarely follows a schedule. It arrives at 2 a.m. when the house is quiet, or on a Tuesday afternoon when a song comes on the radio. For years, the act of remembering the people we've lost has been largely solitary — a private ritual confined to photographs on mantels and memories replayed in the mind. Today, we're changing that with **Memorial Spaces**: a new feature on LastRite that lets families and friends create a shared, living place to gather, share stories, and support one another across distances.

## What Is a Memorial Space?

A Memorial Space is a private, invite-only environment built around a person's legacy. Think of it as a gentle intersection between a family photo album, a guest book, and a community bulletin board — but one that never fades, never gets lost in a move, and never requires someone to host a gathering just to keep the memory alive.

When you create a Memorial Space, you begin with the essentials: the person's name, a photograph, key dates, and a short biography. From there, contributors you invite can add their own photographs, written memories, voice recordings, and even short video tributes. The Space grows richer over time, shaped not by one person's recollection but by the collective voice of everyone whose life was touched.

## Why We Built This

Over the past year, we've spoken with hundreds of families who use LastRite to preserve obituaries and life stories. Again and again, we heard the same thing: the hardest part of grief isn't the initial loss — it's the quiet erasure that follows. Anniversaries pass without acknowledgment. Old friends drift apart. Cousins who never met compare half-remembered stories and wish there were a way to piece them together.

Memorial Spaces was designed to solve exactly that. It gives grief a home — somewhere to return to, not just in the first raw weeks, but on the fifth anniversary, on the person's birthday, on the ordinary days when missing someone feels most acute.

## Key Features at Launch

**Shared Story Feed** — A chronological, collaborative stream where contributors can post memories in text, photo, or audio format. Posts can be reacted to and replied to, turning a collection of tributes into a living conversation.

**Milestone Reminders** — Set recurring reminders for birthdays, anniversaries, and other significant dates. Contributors who opt in receive a gentle notification and an invitation to share something new.

**Privacy Controls** — Every Space is invite-only by default. Admins control who can view, who can contribute, and whether the Space is discoverable.

**Legacy Timeline** — An automatically generated visual timeline that plots contributions alongside key life events, giving viewers a coherent narrative across the years.

## A Note on Sensitivity

We approached the design of Memorial Spaces with the same care we bring to everything at LastRite. There are no advertisements, no algorithmic recommendations, no engagement metrics designed to maximize time-on-site. The Space exists for the people who need it — not for anyone else. Notifications are gentle and optional. The interface is quiet and unhurried.

## Getting Started

Memorial Spaces is available to all LastRite users today. To create your first Space, navigate to your dashboard and select **New Memorial Space**. The setup takes about ten minutes, and you can invite contributors at any point.

We built this feature because we believe remembering someone is an act of love, and love deserves a place it can call home.
